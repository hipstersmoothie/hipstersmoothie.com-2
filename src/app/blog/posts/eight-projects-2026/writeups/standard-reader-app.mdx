`standard.site` data comes in through [Tap](https://atproto.com/blog/introducing-tap) into [Neon](https://neon.tech/) Postgres. For everything *around* an article, I used [microcosm](https://www.microcosm.blue/) instead of building crawlers. [Constellation](https://constellation.microcosm.blue/) takes an article URL and returns what links to it: Bluesky posts, [margin.at](https://margin.at/) notes, Semble cosmik cards, cross-publication citations, cosmik graph edges. That feeds Discussion, "Cited in," related reading, and trending. Comment counts cache locally and refresh from Constellation in the background. [Slingshot](https://slingshot.microcosm.blue/) is the fallback for slow PDS reads (Sifa resume chips on author profiles).

The dev setup was the same as my other projects by this point. Same AI workflow, same stack habits.

RSS is great, and this looks a lot like it. But the open nature of the AT Protocol means you can build a reader without locking into one company's platform. Content isn't walled to a single site; it can move. What that means for businesses building on AT Protocol is still unclear when anyone can index the same records and ship a competing reader.
